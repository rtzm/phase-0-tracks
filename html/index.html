Erik Erikson

This is a page about psychologist Erik Erikson, who is my favorite psychologist.

Why is he my favorite? Here are a few reasons, in no particular order:

  • He was a good writer.
  • He had a very interesting theory about development.
  • He took growth more seriously than Freud.

Now you know why I like him. Here are the stages of his theory of development, taken from Wikipedia:

  1. Hope: trust vs. mistrust (oral-sensory, infancy, 0–2 years)
  2. Will: autonomy vs. shame and doubt (early childhood, 2–4 years)
  3. Purpose: initiative vs. guilt (locomotor-genital, preschool, 4–5 years)
  4. Competence: industry vs. inferiority (latency, school age, 5–12 years)
  5. Fidelity: identity vs. role confusion (adolescence, 13–19 years)
  6. Love: intimacy vs. isolation (early adulthood, 20-39 years)
  7. Care: generativity vs. stagnation (adulthood, 40–64 years)
  8. Wisdom: ego integrity vs. despair (maturity, 65 – death)
